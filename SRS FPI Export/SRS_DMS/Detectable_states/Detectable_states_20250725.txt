The ECU shall detect at least the described features:

 

Blink Duration: the total time from the start to the end of a blink. It is typically measured in units of milliseconds. A challenge associated with blink behavior-based drowsiness detection techniques is the individually-dependent nature of the measure. For instance, some people blink more frequently in wakeful conditions, or some persons' eyes remain slightly open even in sleepy conditions. So, personal calibration is a prerequisite to applying these techniques.

Blink Frequency: the number of blinks per minute. An increased blink frequency is typically associated with the onset of sleep.

Lid Reopening Delay: the measure of the time from fully closed eyelids to the start of their reopening. Its value is in the range of a few milliseconds

 for an awake person, it increases for a drowsy person and is prolonged to several hundred milliseconds for a person undergoing a microsleep.

Microsleep: an eye blink is detected when the upper lid of the eye remains in contact with the lower lid for around 200–400 ms, and if this duration exceeds 500 ms (and less than 10s), this situation corresponds to a microsleep. A driver's microsleep can lead to fatal accidents.

PCR time: the percentage of eye fixations that fall within a predefined road center area during a specific period.

Gaze direction: a vector is generated by observing the driver's pupils and creating from them a vector that approximates the gaze direction. On average, information from both eyes and advanced computer vision techniques are used to recognize the oval, iris, and pupil of the human eye.

Face direction: when the gaze direction is not available, especially when the eyes are occluded, the very same vector is generated approximating the face direction. The latter is done thanks to the nose direction and helped by other important facial features (mouth, face oval and chin).

In cabin 3D reconstruction: both Gaze and Face direction has to be plotted within the cabin geometry in order to recognize if the driver has his eyes on/off road. This information is important in order to estimate the driver attention level.

The Supplier shall guarantee at least the following functionalities:

 

Drowsiness detection: by observing the specifics of the eyes, like closure, movement, etc. ECU shall decide if the driver is in a lowered capacity state of driving, situational awareness, or decision making, giving the ability to the Vehicle to warn the driver and, if needed, stop the Vehicle.

Distraction Detection: give warnings if the eyes are off the road and encourage evasive action. In general, combining eye tracking with forward-facing sensors makes it possible to use this information to warn the driver about potential dangers.

Personalization & Identification: a face recognition system can identify the driver and automatically adjust to user preference settings (music, seat, mirrors, etc.). In addition, the system can be optimized based on eye position.

HMI interaction: control the dashboard and heads-up display using the combination of eyes, hands, and voice. For instance, look at the dashboard or heads-up display and use a scroll wheel and select button on the steering wheel to get a fluid and intuitive experience.

Driver state assessment for Autonomous Driving scenarios: for Autonomous Driving, eye tracking can tell other Vehicle ECUs where the driver is paying attention in order, for example, to trigger automatic engagement of Auto Piloting.
